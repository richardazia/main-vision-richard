The Fashion show:

Fashion shows are fairly easy to shoot, unlike theatre and other productions no story is being told, instead many people walk up and down a cat walk showing the clothes. This allows far more flexibility for the cameraman because he can choose to a certain extent, whether because of a model's beauty or the eccentricity of the clothing. Last year's fashion show was organised so that there were two arms on the stage, one at the centre and another at the end... made it harder to shoot because what the models could do would vary. 

In 2000 the setup was different because the stage was divided in two with a gap along the middle so that models went on both sides of the gap which makes it bad for framing, unless you happen to be shooting in 16:9 to get less wasted space on top and below. When only one model came on it was easier to make the decision on how to shoot it but when two or more came on it became harder, which model to follow etc. When the choice was made as the model comes to the front and can go back the other side it allows you to choose the second model thereby getting one and then the other.

Problems which occured during this shoot were the light and sound, never having good sound... although with an md player plugged into the mixing board it's a little easier to deal with. Another problem is the lights, best solution as in theatre is to decide on the exposure before shooting and keep it locked so that it remains constant throughout. 

While shooting I noticed that many people were passing in front and to a certain extent this is a problem... shortens the usability of the shot yet in another way it's a fade to and from black, perfect for editing without getting jump cuts.
