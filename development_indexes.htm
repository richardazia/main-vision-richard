There are varying views on what a country needs to have in order to be developed and with this variance in view thereby creating the need for different systems to show development.

 

Physical Quality of Life index

The first system of interest is the Physical quality of life index, measured on a scale of 1-100. It's aim is to demonstrate through social indicators that the country is develeoped. Several criterion of the criterion used for this are shown in the table below.

Percentage of population in agriculture infant mortality (per thousand)
literacy rate (%) people per doctor
life expectancy real income (per capita)
school enrolment (% of relevant age group) urban population growth

 

Human development Index

The human development index (HDI) is used because wealth was not seen as a good social indicator, instead it views social welfare. This is concentrated on three main elements 1) adult literacy

2) Real income per capita

3) life expectancy at birth.

It is measured on a scale between 0-1 with one being the highest and zero the lowest.
