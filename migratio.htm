Whether to stay or migrate:

    As with all decisions concerning people and their possesions as well as there way of life people are forced with the prospect of moving to somewhere safer or in other cases for economic reasons

    With examples to actual reasons of international migration I will explain why those factors mentioned in A, B and C are encouraging people to move. These factors which influence people with the desire to move could be those such as in California where Mexicans move in the hope to get a better salary. They then go back to Mexico where they have enough to live on for a while. Meanwhile in such places as the Kosovo where there are whole towns who decide to move it is due to a fraction such as the Serbs which are aggresive towards the local population therefore encouraging them to move to another more welcoming place or simply up into the mountains where the rebel forces won't come to attack. (read NEWSWEEK to find out more)

    People such as Indians used to migrate and some still do from India to countries such as England where they hope to get a better education, therefore getting a better chance at having a western life style and have ammased enough wealth to start a company. However as in the case of Algerians and some other nationalities coming to France they are blocked outside of that country because it(France) doesn't want its own population diluted by an incessant influx of immigrants. This is when the barriers mentioned in B come in. As people find out to their own disaray they sometimes leave their country in the hope for a better life and once they have managed to they can't move back to their country of origin due to such things as famine, civil war and many other factors affecting people's lifes. One area though where people have gained more mobility is from the West to the East and vise versa with the fall of communism and the fall of the Berlin wall in 1989.

    In countries such as France but also such as in Sri Lanka between the Tamil and the Sinhalese there are frictions between the ethnic minorities as well as the locals because they want to split the island so that the minority are in possesion.

    Another more famous example of this is the situation which arose between Israel and Palestine after World War II because the Palestinians were exhiled from their own country which is modern day Israel so that they are living in no man's land where the soil is so poor as to not be sufficient to support the population, therefore this is a barrier created because one country and it's people have invaded another and ejected them from it.

    In a country such as Switzerland if people want to work here there is a barrier which prevents people from staying for long periods of time such as the work permit whcih is only valid a certain amount of time and then the people have to move back to their country of origin before being fined for staying illegaly in a country.

    In an event such as Chernobyl the factor affecting whether people stay in the area or not is the danger of being irradiated by the nuclear waste floating around for thousands of years. People therefore stay away from there not being able to come back since the radioactivity is within the plants and all around the place therefore it is too dangerous to human life.

    In conclusion there are many factors affecting whehter or not people stay within an area but also many factors which help decide whether or not people can stay, for how long and the causes for this decision to move. Some have been voluntary while others have not therefore it is hard to generalise on one particular point.
