The Clip window

 

The clip window is the way in which you view and edit the footage before adding it to the timeline. The window contains a scroll down menu with most editing options.

 

There is a marker option, frame by frame backwards, frame by frame forwards, in point, the play button and the out point button. After these may be found the in point remove button and the outpoint remove button. Also included is the option to select the whole clip as in and out points or to change the speed of the clip.

 

In order to edit the clip in the way you wish for it to be the marker must be placed at the first frame which you would like, at which point it should be marked by pressing the in point button. Play the clip and see where you would like it to end and select the outpoint. In so doing you have performed your first edit and you may drag the clip down to the timeline. You may then repeat this process with the various clips you have captured and through this process build up your sequence.

 

The Timeline

 

The timeline is a graphic representation of the video you are creating. The timeline is divided into various sections. Firstly the top of the timeline window contains many of the keys needed for editing.

 

The second part of the window is dedicated to the video and sound being edited. To the left side can be found V1, A1, A2, A3, A4 etc. These buttons are similar to those found in the record tool and serve the same purpose. As you have noted through editing and bringing down each clip down to the timeline there are a lot of rectangles representing the seperate clips. Sometimes you find that some of the clips you have assembled are too long or too short. In this window you will be able to modify the duration of clips with two methods.

 

Shortening clips:

The first of these methods is by playing the video and arriving towards the clip you would like to shorten. At the beginning of the segment you would like to remove mark your in point. After doing this proceed towards the end of what you want to remove and press out. You will now see that a purple veil is over the part you would like to remove. At this point by cutting out the part you no longer want you have removed the part you did not want.

 

The trim mode:

 

In order to start the trim mode you must click on the trim button which is found top left of the timeline represented as a film with the leader out. By pressing on this window you will enter the trim mode.

 

The trim mode shows the outgoing picture on the left and the incoming picture on the right. There are two displays both displaying zero. These numbers display by how many frames you have moved forwards or backwards.

 

In this mode it is possible to elongate or shorten the outgoing clip by pressing the keys so that you advance one frame at a time or ten. If you click on the left for the outgoing clip you are affecting only the outgoing clip. In clicking on the right button you are affecting only the incoming clip.

 

Another option is through going down to the timeline and when the leader points to the left pull to the left to shorten the outgoing clip. In order to lengthen the outgoing clip and shorten the incoming clip and drag it to the right. Perform the opposite actions to elongate or shorten either clip.

 

Title tool

 

Title tool is the default tool for creating titles. It allows you choice of fonts, colour, position of text and what will be in the background, whether a solid colour or moving video.

 

In order to open this tool it is within the Tools menu under the heading Title tool. Open this window. Once this is done place the cursor at the locations at which you would like to type the text. Once this is done in the keys below the T button is for text, the hand is for moving the text around and the rest is similar to would be used in image and text editing softwares.

Glossary:

Bin: Method of refering to folders where video is contained

Channel: (see track)

Dissolve: To fade from one picture into another.

Extract: To remove a segment so elements come together

In point: Beginning of a clip

Lift: To remove clips but leave a space

Mark: used to locate a segment that may be useful later on

Motion: used to describe the frame rate of video.

Out point: End of clip

Render: To calculate video for effects

Sequence: Used to describe a collection of clips, in this particular software to describe the program being created within the timeline window

Track: Used to describe where the sound and video are located

Trim: To change the duration of a clip
