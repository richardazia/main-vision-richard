WWF/WFN

  1. 24 national organisations
  2. 5 associated organisations
  3. 22 regional offices
  4. programmes in 96 different countries

Aims:

  1. Conserve bio diversities
  2. generate natural resources
  3. to get rid of pollution and wasted energy of natural resources

Priorities:

  1. forests
  2. seas
  3. Ecosystems of fresh water (lakes, rivers, etc.)

Biggest project organised by WWF:

  1. biggest project organised by WWF is the WWF 2000 "The Living Planet Campaign"
  2. The "Caring For The Earth", a new international conservation strategy was launched in 1991. It is sponsored by WWF, IUCN and UNEP. Greenpeace was not involved with this as it works independently.

WWF response to Whale Hunting.

  1. The ten largest whale species were hunted so intensively that some of them may never recover.
  2. In 1998 WWF helped spearhead a landmark resolution through the UN General Assembly that called for the June 1992 moratorium that threatened US sanctions in an attempt to reduce trade related mortality. Japan and Korea agreed to withdraw their driftnet fleets from the South Pacific.
  3. -encourages sustainable fishing.
