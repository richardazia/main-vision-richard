What I meant by brunt and effect is refering to the situation with people sharing terabytes of music with others around the world. When you think about it the artists are not the ones suffering the most from this but the music industry. It is a multi-billion dollar industry based around the complicated pathways required to distribute music.

With the coming of online media and distribution the record comanies can no longer justify part of their costs and must take into consideration the ease with which material may be distributed in comparison when machines for the duplication of music were far more expensive and when quality was lost.

How will a dinosaur like the media industry cope. It's been undercut at the base as have huge mega corporations and the result will be a pile of rubble where once stood the media industry of thirty years ago.

With the World Wide Web and broadband internet access the buyer is becoming a media manager. He decides what style of music he likes, joins forums and groups where others who appreciate the same content are and hence gets the music he wants. This is all at a very reduced cost.

Add to this the advances in personal computing and technology and you'll see that shooting a music clip with a £15,000 camera several years ago can be done with a meer £3000 today. As to editing for a couple of hundred pounds you've got a good system.

Hence the thought that the media industry as a whole may (not saying it will) collapse as a result of file sharing and ease of distribution is an important element of today's society.

In the same way that canals, windmills and furnaces were important during the industrialisation of England and other countries so will the world wide web and the internet's broadband backbone.
