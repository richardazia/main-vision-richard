Recording

 

You can record in two ways:

  • The simpler method is without deck control.
  • The deck control icon is second from right and it is in the right mode when a circle and a line are seen above the deck.

 

Without deck control

 

When record or play out decks are controled by the machine it is referred to as deck control. Recording without deck control means that you control the deck with the play and rewind buttons on the deck. 

 

Before capturing it is Important to check that the correct inputs are selected. These are displayed as the buttons below the V1, A1 etc icons. If only sound is to be recorded then V does not need to be selected and if only video ( V) needs to be recorded then Audio ( A) does not need to be selected.

Once this is done the right bin must be selected from the icons. This is seen as a pull down menu full of options. 

Selecting the disk is next, on this particular system the disk selected should be Media 18GB (G:) and next to this a display with green numbers gives information about how much time can still be recoded onto the hard disk.  

 

With Deck control

 

There are two methods of editing with deck control. The first option is to record one clip at a time whilst the second is to batch digitise. Both methods are practical although for certain projects one method may take precedence over the other.

 

Clip by Clip.

There are two modes of deck controlled recording. The first method is marking an in and out point followed by pressing the record button to record the clip. The process can then be repeated over and over until all the footage is gathered.

 

There is a window with a jog shuttle, fast forward, rewind and play buttons. When capturing these are used to control the deck to get to the clips desired. In order to mark the in and out points there are icons which may be pressed to mark in points and out point.

 

Batch Digitize

 

Batch digitizing is very similar to capturing clip by clip. It allows for a quick going through of footage. In order to do so the mode must be switched towards logging. There are two advantages to logging. The first of these is how fast it may be done, the second is because it gives the editor the freedom not to capture all footage until after all tapes are logged.

 

This is practical as it keeps hard disk usage to a minimum whilst planning an edit. It means that only the useful clips are on the machine whilst giving the flexibility to recuperate them at a later date. Project files themselves hardly take any disk space. What takes space on the hard disks are usually the video files and through using timecode found on betacam and DV tapes a lot of time can be saved at a later date in time.

 

For example imagine that you are creating a summary of the Eurovision song contest and because you need to record several songs with three minutes in length it is useful to go into the logging mode of the record tool. In so doing you may select the in and out points of various clips. After this process is carried out you may find that you have many clips to capture and it may take half an hour for all of them to be captured. You then order the computer to find all the clips on that tape. They will automatically go into the bin you have chosen for them so that once the capture is finished you are ready to edit.
